Pacing Issues Mar a Great Concept

Review of The Last Echo • 5 hours ago

star 3.5

While the sound design is absolutely immersive, particularly in the third act, the first forty-five minutes drag significantly. The characters are well-realized, but the script needed another pass to tighten the dialogue in the mid-section. Still worth seeing on the big screen for the technical achievements alone...

Rarely do we see such a faithful yet modern interpretation of the noir genre. The use of high-contrast lighting is a direct homage to the classics, while the thematic depth addresses contemporary anxieties with surgical precision. It's the film of the year, without a doubt...
